About Me
======
I'm a graduate student in the Physics Department at Stanford University. I grew up in India, earned my Bachelor's in Physics from UCSB, then moved to UMD for my Master's before transferring to Stanford in 2026 to complete my PhD. My research focuses on plasma astrophysics and high-energy astrophysics, using theory and large-scale kinetic and fluid simulations to understand extreme environments around compact objects. I'm also happy to mentor undergraduates who are considering graduate school in physics or astronomy!
</div>

<div class="black-box" markdown="1">
Research Interests
======
My work spans a range of problems in high-energy plasma astrophysics, including:
- Wave propagation and turbulence in strongly magnetized plasmas (e.g. magnetar magnetospheres)
- Accretion physics at the Galactic Center (Sagittarius A*)
- Very high-energy flares and multi-wavelength variability from supermassive black holes
- Modeling multi-wavelength emission from pulsars
- Plasma waves and instabilities 

I use a combination of PIC, force-free and GRMHD simulations, along with analytic theory, to study these systems.
